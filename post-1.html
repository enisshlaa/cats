Four cats were saved from a sinking ship by Thai Navy sailors in the Andaman Sea.

Early in March of 2021, everything came to a head. The ship has received a distress call.

The ship's crew was able to escape before the main body of the vessel was buried in water in a couple of minutes.

Sailing sailors from Thailand's royal navy decided to re-check the overturned vessel for any probable oil spill after rescuing the crew.

Colosseum was nearly fully underwater at this point.

When the soldiers looked at the situation from a safe distance, they were surprised to see four cats.

Tattaphon Sai, a 23-year-old man, sprang into the water and swam to the sinking ship to save the animals.

On his shoulders, a small boy carried all the animals to a navy ship.

Despite the evident risk, the hero declared that he had no intention of abandoning the animals.

Despite their best efforts, they were unable to reach the ground on their own.

On the beach, the animals were taken.

The rescued kitties are still being cared for by the military. Their health and well-being are no longer in jeopardy.
