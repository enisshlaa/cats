When a stranger’s cat was diagnosed with an incurable condition, Reddit users rallied behind her to donate the money she needed to preserve her pet.

The year began with the death of a close family member for Jill. After a long struggle with brain cancer, her brother passed away.

It doesn't end well, either, because the girl is desperately trying to save Ashton, her cat.

Coronaviruses carrying RNA are the primary cause of F.I.P., a serious infectious illness that is sometimes deadly.

Infecting the body's immune cells, the virus then spreads to organs and systems such as the liver, kidneys, spleen, eyes, brain, and spinal cord as a whole.

The medicine has been used to treat this illness in recent years in the United States and in a few European nations.

Although the FDA has not yet approved it, it has already saved numerous lives in the interim.

It was revealed to Jill by the veterinarian that her cat may still be rescued, but the expense of therapy was too exorbitant for her.

So she resorted to Reddit users, pleading with them to assist in extending Ashton's time on this earth for as long as possible.

"I'm at the end of my tether." My life revolves around him," said Jill.

Animal lovers from all around the world took notice of her message right away.

Many of them expressed an interest in sending money to Jill to help pay for the cat's treatment.

Jill was approached by an experienced Reddit member.

He spoke to the young lady on the phone and got all about Ashton's medical history from her.

The man shared a link to a fundraiser he suggested Jill set up on a crowdsourcing site with the rest of the group.

The total amount collected in only a few short hours surpassed the announced goal of $9,000!

Jill expressed her gratitude to everyone who contributed and promised to keep everyone updated on Ashton's treatment progress and expenses.

"It's going to be a long road, but now I have hope because of you." She stated on the collection page, "I am really thankful to each of you."
